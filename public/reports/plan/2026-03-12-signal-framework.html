1 Executive Summary

The SIGNAL Framework is a unified marketing operating system that absorbs the essence of three knowledge streams into one coherent language:

🇰🇷엠타트업 (M-Startup)
15 cohorts of Korean ecommerce marketing wisdom. Core insight: Marketing = Gather + Persuade = RCS. Concepts: Core Value, Purchase Benefits, Marketing Axis, Equivalent Exchange, O-M-C, Traffic Waterway, Choice Control, Keyword Levels, Multiplier Effect.
📊자사몰사관학교 (DTC Academy)
5 cohorts of Meta ads execution mastery. Core formula: Total Value = Ad Spend × Est. Action Rate + Ad Quality. Concepts: 3-Scene Structure, Multiplication Law, Meta Auction 4 Stages, Multiplier Diagnosis.
🌍Global Marketing Science
Schwartz (5 Awareness Stages), Hormozi (Value Equation), Brunson (Value Ladder), Deiss (Customer Value Journey), Godin (Purple Cow), Suby (8-Phase Selling), AARRR (Pirate Metrics).

What makes SIGNAL different: It doesn't just combine these — it creates a new language with one root metaphor ("Signal and Amplification"), adds AI as a fundamental 4th axis (not a tool), and introduces Signal DNA — a compounding data flywheel that makes each practitioner's marketing uncopyable over time.

SIGNAL operates on four axes: Resource (what you have) × Channel (where you go) × Persuasion (what you say) × AI Co-pilot (who assists). Above all four sits Signal DNA — the meta-layer that turns generic AI outputs into your unique competitive advantage.

2 The Core Design Problem

Three Frameworks, Three Languages

Before SIGNAL, practitioners had to mentally juggle three different metaphor families:

SourceMetaphor FamilyExample TermsProblem
엠타트업InfrastructureRCS, 물길 (Waterway), 시스템 7대영역Engineering language — powerful but cold
자사몰사관학교Formula/Math총가치 공식, 곱셈의 법칙, 배수효과Precise but intimidating for beginners
Global (Schwartz, etc.)Funnel/LadderAwareness Stages, Value Ladder, CVJWestern-centric, overlapping terminology
Students and practitioners feel the seams. They learn 등가교환 on Monday, Value Equation on Tuesday, and don't realize they're the same concept in different clothes.

The Solution: One Root Metaphor

The best frameworks feel like they're describing one coherent reality from different angles. SIGNAL chose "Signal and Amplification" from physics/engineering as its root metaphor:

The Signal Journey: Create a signal (value) → Broadcast through channels → Amplify with persuasion → Automate with AI → Compound with data (Signal DNA)

Every concept in the framework lives naturally within this metaphor. No "metaphor buffet" — just one world.

Design Rules

  • Two syllables max for core nouns — must flow in team meetings
  • Action verb pairing — every term maps to "what do I do Monday morning?"
  • Enemy per axis — each axis fights a specific enemy (Invisibility → Obscurity → Skepticism → Friction)
  • Narrative spine — the framework is a plot, not a checklist

3 The Four Axes — RCPA

SIGNAL operates on four axes. Each axis has an enemy to defeat, a core question, and 3-4 key terms. Together they cover the entire marketing operation.

R
Resource
Enemy: Invisibility — "We have something, but nobody can see it"
"What do we actually have?"
Signal Stack
Everything your team owns — product, story, people, time, money, content. Your total inventory of assets before any marketing begins.
Replaces: RCS Resource layer
→ Hormozi's "Dream Outcome ingredients", Brunson's offer stack
Signal Mass
The one thing about your product that competitors cannot easily copy. The core of your competitive advantage. If you can't name it in one sentence, you don't have it yet.
Replaces: 핵심가치 (Core Value), 핵심가치 이동
→ Godin's Purple Cow (remarkability), Hormozi's Dream Outcome
Proof Assets
Tangible evidence that your Signal Mass is real — reviews, demos, before/after photos, user testimonials, certifications. Not claims. Evidence.
Replaces: 구매편익 4분류 (Purchase Benefit Types)
→ Suby's "Godfather Offer" proof elements, Schwartz's "Most Aware" stage content
C
Channel
Enemy: Obscurity — "We exist, but nobody encounters us"
"Where do our customers already look?"
Flow Map
The exact path from a customer's first encounter with you to purchase. Not theoretical — draw it. Every step, every click, every decision point.
Replaces: 물길 (Traffic Waterway), 물길 마인드맵
→ Brunson's funnel stages, AARRR Acquisition→Activation, Deiss CVJ steps 1-4
Flow Source
Where attention originates. Two types: Organic (earned — content, SEO, community) and Paid (bought — Meta ads, Google ads). Your Flow Map starts here.
Replaces: 제로마케팅 (Zero Marketing) vs 페이드마케팅
→ Suby's 3%/17%/20%/60% market split, 채널(C) 전략
Flow Gate
The moment in your Flow Map where you narrow options and force a decision — "click here", "add to cart", "choose A or B". Strategic friction that converts browsers to buyers.
Replaces: 선택제어 3종 (Choice Control: Easy Choice / Double Bind / Elimination)
→ Behavioral economics "choice architecture", 클로징 4요소
P
Persuasion
Enemy: Skepticism — "They see us, but don't believe us"
"Why should they believe us?"
Signal Arc
The 3-beat persuasion structure: Hook (stop scrolling) → Proof (show evidence) → Claim (make the ask). This single concept absorbs O-M-C, 3-Scene Structure, AND Schwartz's awareness progression.
Replaces: O-M-C (Opening/Merit/Closing), 3장면 구조 (3-Scene Structure)
→ Schwartz's awareness-to-action arc, Suby's 8-Phase selling sequence
Gravity Score
The perceived fairness of the exchange. Does what the customer gets feel worth more than what they pay? High gravity = irresistible pull. Not about being cheap — about designing the exchange so value obviously exceeds cost.
Replaces: 등가교환 (Equivalent Exchange), 매몰비용
→ Hormozi's Value Equation: (Dream Outcome × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)
Desire Axis
The spectrum of customer intent: Latent (don't know they want it) → Active (searching for solutions) → Ready (credit card in hand). Your Signal Arc must match where they are on this axis.
Replaces: 마케팅축 (Marketing Axis: Desire/Alternative/Access), 관여도
→ Schwartz 5 Awareness Stages, 엠타트업 키워드 5단계
Scene Stack
The three visual/narrative moments that make up a single piece of content or ad: Problem (pain) → Transformation (aha!) → Aspiration (desire). The visual layer of your Signal Arc.
Replaces: 3장면 구조 (Problem Scene → Transition Scene → Ideal Scene)
→ 싱귤래리티 효과 (focus on one person's story)
A
AI Co-pilot
Enemy: Friction & Scale — "It works, but we can't do it fast enough"
"What can we automate right now?"
Pilot Brief
The structured prompt you give AI before any task. Not a vague ask — a brief that includes context, constraints, examples, and success criteria. This is a NEW concept — no source equivalent.
NEW — no direct predecessor
→ Prompt engineering meets project management. The quality of your Pilot Brief determines the quality of every AI output.
Amplify Loop
The process of turning one proven asset into many variants using AI. One good ad → 10 headline tests. One customer review → 5 content angles. Amplification, not creation from scratch.
Replaces: 배수효과 (Multiplier Effect)
→ 곱셈의 법칙 (Multiplication Law): CTR × Retention × Checkout × Purchase. Each 10% improvement = 46% total growth.
Signal Score
An AI-readable composite metric that combines spend efficiency + creative quality + conversion performance. Your north-star number. When Signal Score goes up, your marketing is working.
Replaces: 총가치 공식 (Total Value = Ad Spend × Est. Action Rate + Ad Quality)
→ Meta Auction mechanics, 배수효과 진단 (Multiplier Diagnosis: benchmark / current = priority)
Keyword Ladder
The five stages of search intent, from pure information to ready-to-buy. Each rung requires different content: Level 5 (browse) → Level 4 (problem) → Level 3 (compare) → Level 2 (evaluate) → Level 1 (purchase).
Replaces: 키워드 5단계, 접촉지점
→ Schwartz 5 Awareness Stages (nearly 1:1 mapping), Suby's 3%/17%/20%/60% market split

4 Signal DNA — The Meta-Layer

"AI marketing without your data is the internet's average. Your Signal DNA makes your marketing uncopyable."

The Problem with Generic AI

Ask any AI to "write a cleansing foam ad" and you get the average of every cleansing foam ad on the internet. It's fine. It's also what everyone else gets. Zero competitive advantage.

The Signal DNA Formula

AI Base Output + Your Accumulated Data = Your Signal DNA
(reference aggregate, same for everyone) + (unique, non-replicable) = (output competitors cannot reproduce)

Signal DNA sits above the four RCPA axes — not as a 5th axis, but as an operating layer. Think of it this way:

RCPA axes = your muscles (what you do). Signal DNA = the neural pathways that make those muscles stronger over time (what you learn).

The Signal Flywheel

Signal DNA File — Structure

A living document (Google Doc or Notion) that every team maintains. Four sections mirror the four axes:

RResource Log
Assets created, product insights discovered, Signal Mass refinements. "What do we know about our product that we didn't know last week?"
CChannel Log
Reach, CTR, traffic by source. Which Flow Sources work? Which Flow Gates convert? "Where are our customers actually coming from?"
PPersuasion Log
Winning hooks, effective Signal Arcs, Gravity Score experiments. "Which words and images make people stop and buy?"
AAI Prompt Library
Best Pilot Briefs, successful prompt templates, AI tool workflows. "Which prompts produce the best outputs for our specific product?"

Signal Debrief — The Weekly Ritual

Every Friday, 15 minutes. Not a grand event — a small, repeating ritual that compounds.

Signal Debrief Template (15 min): 1. What did we run this week? (actions taken) 2. What did the data say? (numbers, not feelings) 3. What surprised us? (unexpected wins or losses) 4. What's our next move? (specific, testable) 5. Update the DNA File (log it or lose it)
Rituals beat events. A weekly 15-minute debrief compounds more learning than a monthly 2-hour review. The Signal DNA File is where institutional memory lives — when a team member leaves, the knowledge stays.

Key Insight: The 1:1 Mapping

Schwartz's 5 Awareness Stages (1966) maps almost perfectly to 엠타트업's Keyword 5 Levels:

Unaware = Level 5 (Pure Information) · Problem Aware = Level 4 (Problem Recognition) · Solution Aware = Level 3 (Alternative Search) · Product Aware = Level 2 (Comparison) · Most Aware = Level 1 (Ready to Buy)

Two frameworks, developed independently on opposite sides of the world, 60 years apart — arriving at the same insight. Marketing's essence is universal. SIGNAL just names it once.

8 Application: 15-Week University Course

This is one application of the SIGNAL Framework. The framework itself is universal — applicable to startups, agencies, corporate teams, or solo practitioners. This section shows how to deploy it as a hands-on university course.

Course Overview

Cosmetics Customer-Based Marketing Practice · 15 weeks · 4 hours/week · Teams of 4-5 · Real cosmetic products

Weekly Cadence

Every Week: Monday → Draft Pilot Brief (plan + AI objectives) Wednesday → Execute (create + deploy content/ads) Friday → Signal Debrief (15 min → update DNA File)

Grading Philosophy

Execution and learning > theoretical perfection. Failed experiments with documented learnings earn higher marks than safe, limited plans. The goal is 50+ experiments in the DNA File — volume of learning, not just success.

9 The Cross-Cohort Moat

The SIGNAL Framework's most powerful feature isn't visible in any single cohort — it's what happens across cohorts.

Cohort 1
🌱 Raw Data
Teams generate original campaign data, unique prompt libraries, winning hooks and angles. First Signal DNA Files are created from scratch.
→
Cohort 2
🌿 Baseline Brief
Inherits anonymized best practices: proven hooks, channel benchmarks, validated Pilot Brief templates. Starts higher, goes further.
→
Cohort 3+
🌳 Compounding IP
Industry-specific cosmetics data + curated prompt libraries = unreplicable advantage. The framework itself gets smarter every semester.
"You can copy the curriculum. You cannot copy three cohorts of validated data, refined prompts, and battle-tested Signal DNA."

Each cycle of the flywheel makes the next one more powerful. This is the instructor's IP moat.
